Case Study (Phishing)

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. This is usually done through fake emails or websites that appear to be legitimate.

A typical phishing attack begins when a user receives a message claiming to be from a trusted organization, such as a bank. The message urges the user to click a link to verify their account. The link leads to a fake website designed to look real. Once the user enters their details, the attacker gains access to their information.

In India, phishing attacks often target users through fake UPI links and SMS alerts. Students and professionals are common targets due to frequent online activity.

The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to accounts. Awareness and caution are essential to prevent such attacks.
